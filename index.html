01 — 2025

Fine-tuning an LLM to tell Kazakh stories.

A small-scale SFT + DPO pipeline adapting an open Llama-3.1 model to generate structured Kazakh-language children's stories.

Repo →

Question

Can a Llama-3.1 base model be adapted, on consumer hardware, to produce structurally coherent Kazakh-language stories — when public training data in Kazakh is sparse and most NLP tooling is built around English?

Approach

  • Scraped 1,000+ Kazakh-language stories from three sources; applied MinHash deduplication and cleaned the output into a curated training corpus.
  • Built a 240-story annotated corpus with control tags (age, theme, length, seed); manually annotated 40 stories with structured gold plans.
  • Implemented SFT and DPO post-training on Llama-3.1-KazLLM-1.0-8B via QLoRA (4-bit quantization). DPO preference pairs were constructed using BERTScore F1 against the gold plans (xlm-roberta-base, lang='kk').
  • Evaluated three models against each other with automatic metrics, human judgement on a small rubric, and LLM-as-judge (GPT-4, Gemini).

Why it matters for DACSS

Most NLP tooling, datasets, and methodological norms are built around English. Building reliable pipelines for a low-resource language like Kazakh is a prerequisite for any serious NLP application to Central Asian text — news, public records, oral histories. This project is a small step in that direction.

02 — 2025

Does spending on education predict math-olympiad outcomes?

A country-year predictive model linking World Bank education indicators to International Mathematical Olympiad results.

RtidymodelsLightGBMtidyverse
Repo →
Model performance vs. baseline
LightGBM · 5-fold CV

Question

How much of a country's performance at the International Mathematical Olympiad can be explained by structural education indicators — public spending on education, enrolment rates, GDP per capita — and how much is essentially noise?

Approach

  • Constructed a country–year panel dataset linking World Bank education and macro indicators to IMO results, with careful entity resolution across data sources and structured handling of missingness.
  • Tuned a LightGBM regressor using R + tidymodels with cross-validation; compared against a linear-regression baseline.
  • Inspected variable importance and partial-dependence patterns to identify which indicators carry signal beyond GDP.

§ 03 / About

Background.

I spent ten years as a backend engineer at Halyk Bank and ATF Bank — Kazakhstan's largest bank and one of its top commercial banks. The work was mostly in regulated financial systems: a CQRS / Kafka migration that cut mortgage approval time from two weeks to two or three days, a RabbitMQ event mesh that brought loan-issuance latency down by about 70%, a Go-and-Kafka platform that onboarded a thousand-plus enterprise clients onto a previously paper-based tender process.

I came to DACSS to broaden the data-and-ML side of that work — to build modeling, NLP, and analytics skills on top of the engineering foundation, with the time and structure of a graduate program to do it carefully. The projects on this page sit at that seam: a low-resource NLP pipeline on Kazakh, a predictive model on a country-panel education dataset, and the engineering scaffolding (ETL, REST APIs, tests) that lets any of it be reproduced.
